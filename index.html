A/B Test Uber — Clearer Pricing Estimate

Uber initially experimented with displaying a price range ($14–$20) for rides to better manage traffic and costs. However, users often focused on the higher end of the range, leading to uncertainty and a decline in bookings. To address this, Uber switched to showing a single estimated price, aiming to reduce hesitation and build trust by providing a clearer expectation of ride costs.

Version A — Before

Price range creates uncertainty

Users see a price range like US$13–17 for UberX. While this gives flexibility, users tend to anchor on the higher end of the range, creating anxiety about the final cost and reducing booking confidence.

Version B — After

Single price builds trust

Users see a clear single price like $10.99 for UberX with estimated arrival time and a "Cheaper" badge. This removes ambiguity, reduces decision fatigue, and creates a more confident booking experience.

Results

Double-digit increase in rides per user

By switching from price ranges to single estimated prices, Uber saw a significant boost in ride frequency. The clearer pricing reduced user hesitation and built trust, resulting in more bookings per user.

A/B Test TikTok · Delayed Bank Account Setup

TikTok sought to boost enrollment in its revenue-sharing program, which required creators to complete a KYC process, including linking their bank accounts, before they could earn revenue. The initial process caused a significant drop-off, as creators were hesitant to provide banking details upfront. To address this, TikTok revised the onboarding flow, allowing creators to start producing content and see potential earnings before connecting their bank accounts. This change aimed to reduce friction and motivate more creators to join the program, easing the initial commitment and building trust.

Version A — Before

Upfront bank account requirement blocks momentum

Creators land on a "Monthly earnings" page showing $0.00 with no records. The interface requires bank account setup before any content creation can begin. This creates a high-friction entry point that discourages new creators from completing the enrollment process.

Version B — After

Delayed setup lets creators build momentum first

Creators can explore the Creativity Program, see available rewards programs, and start producing content before linking a bank account. The flow shows earning potential upfront — Creator Rewards, Video Gifts, Subscriptions — building motivation before asking for commitment.

Results

25% increase in creators enrolling in the program

By delaying the bank account requirement and letting creators see earning potential first, TikTok significantly reduced onboarding friction and increased program enrollment.

A/B Test Duolingo · Decoupling Streaks

Duolingo redesigned its streak feature to better support user engagement and retention. Previously, maintaining a streak required users to meet their daily learning goals, which could be daunting for those with higher goals. The redesign decoupled streaks from daily goals, allowing users to preserve their streak by completing just one lesson per day, regardless of their set goal. This change aimed to reduce pressure and make streaks more achievable, particularly for learners with ambitious goals.

Version A — Before

Streaks require meeting full daily goals

Users must complete their entire daily XP goal to maintain their streak. For learners with higher goals, this creates pressure and anxiety. Missing a single day resets the streak entirely, which can be demotivating and lead to disengagement.

Version B — After

One lesson keeps the streak alive

Streaks are preserved by completing just one lesson per day, regardless of the daily goal. This celebrates consistency over intensity. The new "Lesson complete!" and "day streak!" screens provide positive reinforcement, making daily practice feel rewarding rather than stressful.

Results

Increase in the number of learners on a 7+ day streak by over 40%

By decoupling streaks from daily goals, Duolingo made streaks more achievable and reduced the anxiety of breaking them. This led to significantly more users maintaining consistent learning habits.

UX Breakdown Tesla Vehicle Dashboard

A comprehensive UX breakdown of the Tesla mobile app's vehicle dashboard — analyzing how identity, visualization, actions, alerts, and navigation come together in a single screen.

Microinteractions

The top section delivers identity and status instantly

The driver name, battery level, and car state sit right at the top, so users can anchor themselves within a second. The tap interaction that lets you switch between battery percentage and mileage is a tiny detail that removes friction and respects how different people think about range.

Real-World Match

3D car model mirrors real-world state

Tesla shows your exact car color, wheel type, and door status. The model updates based on real conditions, so it doubles as a status report. This frees users from reading heavy labels and lets the visual do most of the interpretation.

Call to Action

High frequency actions live directly under the car

Lock, climate, charging, and trunk controls sit right below the model because that's the moment users naturally look for interaction. The placement matches real-world intent, which cuts down on searching and creates a smooth mental flow.

Notification

Contextual system alerts stay visible

Software updates, phone key setup, and other system prompts appear only when they matter. They sit in their own block instead of mixing with controls, which keeps urgency clear without hijacking the layout.

Information Architecture

Navigation groups actions into clean categories

Controls, climate, location, and summon are grouped in a compact list with simple icons and short subtitles. The structure reduces cognitive load and helps users predict where each action will live, even before reading the labels.

UX Breakdown Task Board

A comprehensive UX breakdown of a task management board — analyzing how grouping, card design, navigation, analytics, and minimal UI come together to help users stay focused and organized.

Information Architecture

Task grouping creates a scannable structure

Tasks are divided into clear stages like To Do, This Week, and Review. This grouping creates a natural structure that helps users quickly understand what needs attention now versus later. Instead of showing a long unstructured list, the board format organizes work into meaningful categories. This reduces cognitive load and helps users prioritize faster.

Visual Hierarchy

Task cards balance density and readability

Each task card contains multiple details such as assignee avatars, due dates, subtasks, comments, and reminders. Even with this amount of information, the design keeps everything readable through subtle typography and icon usage. Important details like deadlines are highlighted while secondary information stays quiet. This hierarchy lets users scan many tasks quickly without feeling overwhelmed.

Metrics

Insights panel gives a high level understanding of progress

The Insights panel on the right provides analytics about project health, progress, and completed tasks. Instead of forcing users to analyze every task manually, the dashboard summarizes key signals. Visual indicators such as progress bars, activity graphs, and risk warnings help users quickly understand the state of the project and decide where attention is needed.

Call to Action

Sidebar organizes navigation with clear priority

The left sidebar separates global navigation, personal views, and project spaces. Icons and spacing create clear visual grouping which makes navigation feel predictable and easy to scan. The Add task button stands out as the main action of the product. Highlighting the primary action ensures users can quickly capture tasks without searching for the entry point.

Minimalism

Minimal UI keeps the focus on the work itself

The overall interface uses a restrained color palette, generous spacing, and subtle dividers. This minimal approach prevents visual noise and keeps attention on the tasks rather than the interface. For productivity tools this is important. When the interface stays quiet, users can focus on organizing and completing their work without distraction.

UX Breakdown Search & Discover

A comprehensive UX breakdown of a stock trading discover screen — analyzing how search, index snapshots, trending content, segmented movers, and subtle alerts work together to help users explore the market efficiently.

Clarity

Market status + region toggle reduce cognitive friction

The green Open indicator instantly answers a high intent question before the user even searches. When multiple markets are supported, this prevents costly mistakes. The Aus and Wall St toggle at the top reinforces control. Users decide the context before they explore, not after. This is strong contextual framing. It reduces mental switching and keeps discovery focused.

Information Architecture

Index snapshots deliver high value in low space

Indexes are compact, horizontally arranged, and paired with micro live charts. In one glance, users see performance and direction. The small sparkline preview removes the need to tap into each index just to understand momentum. It respects time and reduces unnecessary navigation.

Retention

Trending today drives discovery beyond intent

Most users open a trading app without a specific ticker in mind. Trending today captures that moment. It nudges exploration and surfaces opportunities users may not have considered. This increases session depth and engagement without feeling pushy. Discovery becomes part of the product, not an afterthought.

Real-World Match

Movers segmented into Most up and Most down

Instead of one long volatile list, the screen splits movers into clear categories. This reduces scanning effort and speeds decision making. The structure mirrors how investors think. Gainers and losers. It matches mental models, which makes the interface feel intuitive. Clear grouping always beats long lists.

Minimalism

Subtle stock alerts without visual noise

Small message icons beside stock names quietly signal announcements or major updates. There are no loud banners or heavy labels. Just a minimal indicator that rewards attentive users. It informs without overwhelming. That balance between awareness and cleanliness is hard to get right.

UX Breakdown AI Workspace

A comprehensive UX breakdown of an AI-powered workspace — analyzing how familiar chat patterns, contextual sources, flexible input methods, personalized files, and minimal layout work together to create an intelligent and focused experience.

Affordance

Follow established AI chat patterns to reduce friction

Instead of inventing a new interaction model, the interface follows familiar AI chat conventions. Input at the bottom, conversation in the center, context on the side. This lowers cognitive load and helps users focus on what matters: the prompt and the response.

Information Architecture

Context lives beside the conversation, not inside it

The Sources panel sits on the right, separate from the main chat. Users can upload files, browse content, or reference documents without breaking their flow. Keeping context adjacent but not embedded prevents clutter and keeps the conversation readable.

User Control

Multiple input paths increase perceived intelligence

The Sources card supports file upload, image upload, browsing content, and @ tagging. This creates flexibility in how users provide context. When the system adapts to different input behaviors, it feels more capable and responsive.

Personalization

Personalized related files build trust in the AI

The Related section surfaces documents connected to the current query. This small detail signals that the AI understands context beyond a single message. It reduces search effort and makes the experience feel tailored.

Minimalism

Minimal UI with controllable layout keeps focus intact

The interface uses restrained color and generous spacing to prioritize content. Side panels can be collapsed, giving users control over complexity. This balance between simplicity and customization supports both beginners and power users.
